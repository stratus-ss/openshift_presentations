Container Management

* Containers are just isolated Linux Namespaces

* Any Linux OS can serve as the base for K8s

* Since they are just NS, they all share the same host resources (including kernel)

* K8s has a liveness daemon that will migrate or restart a pod when problems occur

Explained in 6 Minutes

* K8s has an API, a scheduler and ETCD for tracking the state of the cluster

* K8s deploys pods, not individual containers

* Everything in a pod communicates over local host just like a VM with multiple services

* Configuration, certs, ENV vars and files can be mounted to a pod

* Config maps are plane text

* Secrets are base64 encoded
